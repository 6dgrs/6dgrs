Meet With Trust

6dgrs is built around mutual paths, controlled visibility, and verified real-world interactions.

How 6dgrs keeps trust

People are discovered through Trusted Friends and mutual paths, not open public feeds.

Meetup safety tips

Meet in public places, keep plans small, share context with trusted friends, and only unlock networks after a real meetup.

Verification methods

Use QR scan, same-location checks, or timed mutual confirmation to prove a meetup happened in person.

Reporting and blocking

Reports stay private. Blocking removes the person from your visible trusted graph.

After a verified meetup

Meetups create Met first. Wider network unlock only happens when both people accept a Trusted Friend relationship and a slot is available.

The Network

Trusted FriendDirect trusted circle

Someone you know personally or have built trust with over time. Trusted Friends unlock network visibility, introduce you to people they know, vouch for you, and join plans with you.

Limited slotsStrongest connectionUnlocks routes
Mutual ConnectionYou -> Lily -> Emma

Someone connected to you through an active trust path, such as a Trusted Friend or someone you have Met. Mutuals can be discovered and introduced to, but direct messaging waits until an introduction or real-world meetup is confirmed.

DegreeHow close someone is
1stTrusted Friend2ndFriend of a Trusted Friend3rdFriend of a Mutual Connection4thMultiple trust paths5thExtended Network6thDistant Network Connection

The further out the degree, the lighter the visibility and the more curated the access.

MetConfirmed in real life

Someone you have met through a plan, intro, QR, or location confirmation. Met connections are unlimited, can be messaged, and can open curated branch previews, but they do not use a Trusted Friend slot or automatically unlock the full branch.

VouchedRecommended by trust

A personal signal from someone in your trusted circle. Vouches can make a person easier to discover or introduce, but they are still separate from becoming a Trusted Friend.

Archived PathHistory, not access

A relationship or route you had before, but no longer actively unlocks. Past chats, plans and meetup history remain available, while wider network access closes.

Locked NetworkNo active path

A person, city hub, plan, or branch that cannot currently be accessed because the trust path is missing or broken. Locked branches can show hints, but not full profiles, direct messaging, private trips, or full branch access.

How Networks Unlock

Meeting unlocks connection. Trust unlocks access.

Request Introduction is for someone you have not met yet. After a real meetup is confirmed, that person becomes Met first. If trust builds and you both confirm the relationship, they can become a Trusted Friend and open a wider branch.

Meetup Confirmations

Confirming a real-world meetup moves a person from Connected or Shared Plan to Met. Met gives shared history and curated previews; Trusted Friend opens the wider branch.
